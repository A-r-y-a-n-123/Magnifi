Breaking Bad

Breaking Bad is an American television drama series created by Vince Gilligan, which originally aired from 2008 to 2013. The show follows Walter White (played by Bryan Cranston), a high school chemistry teacher who turns to manufacturing and selling methamphetamine after being diagnosed with terminal cancer. His transformation from a mild-mannered teacher into a ruthless drug kingpin, under the alias Heisenberg, is at the heart of the series. Known for its intense storytelling, complex characters, and moral dilemmas, Breaking Bad received critical acclaim and is widely regarded as one of the greatest television shows of all time.

Game of thrones

Game of Thrones is a highly acclaimed fantasy drama series based on George R.R. Martin’s book series, A Song of Ice and Fire. It aired on HBO from 2011 to 2019, spanning eight seasons. Set in the fictional continents of Westeros and Essos, the series follows several noble families as they vie for control of the Iron Throne and the Seven Kingdoms. The show is known for its complex characters, intricate political plots, and shocking twists, including the brutal deaths of major characters. Key houses include the Starks, Lannisters, and Targaryens, each with their own rivalries, alliances, and ambitions.

Better Call Saul

Better Call Saul is an American television series created by Vince Gilligan and Peter Gould, and is a prequel to the acclaimed show Breaking Bad. It aired from 2015 to 2022, spanning six seasons. The series focuses on the transformation of Jimmy McGill (played by Bob Odenkirk), a struggling public defender, into the morally compromised criminal lawyer Saul Goodman, a character made famous in Breaking Bad. Set in the early 2000s, the show explores Jimmy's relationships with his older brother Chuck McGill (Michael McKean), who is a successful lawyer, and his eventual descent into the morally gray world of legal manipulation and criminal defense.

Interstellar

Interstellar is a 2014 science fiction film directed by Christopher Nolan and co-written by Nolan and his brother Jonathan Nolan. The film stars Matthew McConaughey as Cooper, a former NASA pilot who is recruited for a daring space mission to find a new habitable planet for humanity, which is facing extinction due to a global food crisis. The plot explores themes of love, time, gravity, and the survival of the human race, as Cooper and his team travel through a wormhole near Saturn in search of a new home.

Conjuring

The Conjuring is a 2013 supernatural horror film directed by James Wan and produced by New Line Cinema. The film is based on the real-life paranormal investigations of Ed and Lorraine Warren, a married couple of renowned paranormal investigators, portrayed by Patrick Wilson and Vera Farmiga. The story follows the Warrens as they are called to assist the Perron family, who are experiencing terrifying supernatural events in their Rhode Island farmhouse. As the Warrens uncover the dark history of the house, they face a powerful demonic entity that threatens the family’s safety.

Laws

These laws govern the functioning of banks and other financial institutions. They set requirements for capital reserves, lending practices, and liquidity, with the aim of maintaining a stable and secure banking system. Examples include the Banking Act and Basel III regulations, which provide international standards on capital adequacy.

Currency

Fiat Currency: Most modern currencies are fiat currencies, meaning their value is not backed by a physical commodity (like gold or silver) but rather by the trust and authority of the government. Examples include the US Dollar (USD), the Euro (EUR), and the Indian Rupee (INR). Commodity Currency: Historically, many currencies were based on commodities like gold or silver. For example, the gold standard was a system in which a country's currency was directly linked to a specific amount of gold.

Donald Trump

Before his presidency, he was best known for his real estate empire and for hosting the reality TV show "The Apprentice." Hitler

Adolf Hitler was the dictator of Nazi Germany from 1933 to 1945 and the central figure responsible for initiating World War II and the Holocaust. Born in Austria in 1889, Hitler rose to power as the leader of the National Socialist German Workers' Party (Nazi Party), promoting extreme nationalism, racism, and antisemitism. His aggressive foreign policy aimed at expanding German territory led to the invasion of Poland in 1939, sparking the outbreak of World War II.

Putin

Vladimir Putin is the President of Russia, a position he has held in various capacities since 1999, making him one of the longest-serving leaders in modern Russia. Initially serving as Prime Minister, Putin became president in 2000 after the resignation of Boris Yeltsin. His leadership has been marked by a focus on consolidating power, curbing political dissent, and reasserting Russia’s influence on the global stage. Putin has been involved in controversial actions such as the annexation of Crimea in 2014, military interventions in Syria, and the ongoing war in Ukraine (starting in 2022), which has led to widespread international condemnation and sanctions.

Rahul gandhi

Rahul Gandhi is an Indian politician and a prominent member of the Indian National Congress (INC) party, one of India's oldest political organizations. Born into the Nehru-Gandhi family, he is the son of Rajiv Gandhi, former Prime Minister of India, and Sonia Gandhi, the former Congress president. Rahul Gandhi has served as the President of the Indian National Congress from 2017 to 2019 and is currently a Member of Parliament. He has been an advocate for economic reforms, social justice, and inclusive development, although his leadership has faced criticism for the Congress party's performance in recent elections.
